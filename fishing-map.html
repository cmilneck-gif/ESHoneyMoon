What to know before you fish

These notes are based on current AGFC rules and pages available on March 27, 2026. Because your trip is April 20-24, 2026, I weighted the items that matter in late April around Beaver Tailwater.

Beaver Tailwater basics
A trout permit is required there, and you already have the right permit. The Beaver Tailwater is the White River stretch from 100 yards below Beaver Dam down to Houseman Access.
  • One rod only on Beaver Tailwater: AGFC says anglers may use no more than one fishing rod or pole there, and it must be attended at all times.
  • Do not fish within 100 yards of Beaver Dam: fishing, boating, and wading are prohibited inside that zone.
  • Bait on Beaver Tailwater: if you use bait, it must be on a single hooking point. In the signed artificial-only stretch below the dam, natural or scented bait is prohibited and all hooking points must be barbless.
  • Parker Bend matters: the artificial-lures-only zone runs from about 0.5 mile below Beaver Dam to about 100 yards upstream of Parker Bend Access. That means Parker Bend itself is the safer beginner pick if you want to relax with bait rather than fish artificial-only water.
  • Current trout limit change: AGFC's emergency trout order says anglers on Beaver Tailwater may keep only 2 trout up to 14 inches and must release all other trout. Since you are planning catch-and-release, the main practical takeaway is to release any trout over 14 inches immediately and handle fish gently.
  • Houseman is farther downstream: it is still public Beaver Tailwater access, but it sits away from the tighter near-dam regulation area and can feel calmer when you mainly want room to learn.
  • Night rule: only rainbow trout may be harvested from 30 minutes after sunset to 30 minutes before sunrise on Beaver Tailwater.
  • Your travel dates overlap snagging season: from April 15 through June 15, snagging game fish is legal only in the near-dam stretch down to the first Corps ramp. That can concentrate people closer to the dam, which is why Parker Bend is my quieter trout recommendation.
Always check water release before tailwater fishing
Call the Southwestern Power Administration generation line at 866-494-1993 before you head to Beaver Tailwater. Water levels can change quickly and unsafe conditions can develop fast.
Catch-and-release for learning
Wet your hands before touching fish, keep them in the water as much as possible, and pinch down barbs when you can. That is especially helpful for trout and for a new angler still getting comfortable with unhooking fish.
Spot-specific caution
  • Dam Site Park: scenic trout water, but it sits closer to the more regulated and potentially busier near-dam zone. Best if you are comfortable using small lures or flies instead of bait.
  • Kings River: if you explore it, know that special smallmouth limits apply on parts of the river. Since you are releasing fish, the easy rule is simply release all bass there.
  • Black Bass Lake: respect posted restricted areas around the dam and spillway, and stay on the public shoreline and trail-side bank areas.
