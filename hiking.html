What to know before you hike

These notes are based on current official trail and land-manager pages available on March 27, 2026. I weighted the items that matter most for your April 20-24, 2026 trip near Eureka Springs.

Your simplest nearby plan
If you want low-stress trail time close to town, start with Black Bass Lake or Lake Leatherwood. If you want a memorable day trip, Lost Valley is the easiest waterfall hike, Whitaker Point is the iconic overlook, and White Rock is the quietest big-view option.
  • Lost Valley is day-use only: the National Park Service says the Lost Valley area is open for day use only, and backcountry camping is not permitted on the Lost Valley Trail.
  • No dogs on Lost Valley: Buffalo National River's current pets page says pets are not allowed on most park trails, and Lost Valley is not one of the dog-allowed exceptions.
  • White Rock bluff surfaces are currently restricted: the Forest Service has an active order through December 31, 2026 prohibiting people from being on the rock surface of the White Rock bluffs. Stay on the designated rim trail and overlooks instead of scrambling out onto the bluff faces.
  • Black Bass dam and spillway are no-access areas: Eureka Springs Parks specifically notes that the dam, the area below the dam, and the spillway area are closed to access.
  • Lake Leatherwood trails are non-motorized: Visit Eureka Springs notes that the park's trails are closed to motorized vehicles.
  • Whitaker Point road note: the Forest Service lists the trailhead as open 24 hours and free, but the last stretch in is still a forest road. The agency also posted January 2026 weekday construction work at the trailhead that was expected through the end of February, so I would still glance at current alerts before your April trip.
  • White River hiking is limited: near your cabin, the river corridor is much better for scenic access points and short walks than for long formal trails. Beaver Trail is the clearest actual trail close to that side of your trip.
  • Hobbs hunting-season closures do not affect your April trip: the Arkansas State Parks closure dates listed are in November, December, and January, so Sinking Stream and Van Winkle should be a safer spring backup.
April reality check
Expect slick rocks, spring mud, ticks, and spotty service on the bigger drives. Bring water, do not count on cell coverage for navigation in the forest, and turn around early rather than forcing a trail after rain.
Good beginner pacing
Black Bass Lake, Lake Leatherwood, Passion Play, and Hobbs are the easiest trails to pair with a relaxed trip day. Lost Valley is the most forgiving scenic day trip. Whitaker Point and White Rock are better once you are in the mood for a longer drive and rougher approach roads.
Spot-specific caution
  • Black Bass Lake: no facilities on-site, and the dam / spillway area is restricted.
  • Lake Leatherwood: the trail system is large and mixed-use, so it is easy to turn a casual walk into a longer outing than planned.
  • Passion Play / Marble Flats: this is a multi-use trail system with bikes in the mix, so stay alert at intersections and faster downhill sections.
  • Whitaker Point: the overlook is exposed, the trail can feel busier than it looks online, and the approach road is the bigger headache for most visitors.
  • White Rock: the views are excellent, but this is still a cliff-edge area with an active bluff-surface closure and a pack-it-in / pack-it-out feel.
